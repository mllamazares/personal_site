## mens-what?

Mensa is this organization for people with high IQs. To enter you need to take a in-person test and score in the top 2% (98th percentile), which is two-sigmas away from the mean.

It started in the 40s in the UK and started in Spain around the 80s. Since then there are tons of replications in different countries. Their mission can be summarized as:
1. identify and promote human intelligence for the benefit of humanity.
2. encourage research on the nature, characteristics, and uses of intelligence.
3. provide a stimulating intellectual and social environment for its members.

## the test

I did the Mensa online test, then the paid online version called T2, which is supposed to be more accurate. Since I got decent results in both I decided to give it a try at the in-person test. They arranged a session in a public library. Two days later I got accepted! 

Something to highlight is that the exams are culture fair. Mensa is not political, sex or culture biased. The exam aims to capture your raw fluency identifying patterns in different contexts, abstractions to relate ideas. Everything in a limited ammount of time, so you virtually have some seconds to connect the dots and make a decision.

## iq is not everything

I'm not a big believer in IQ as a the ultimate indicator of pure intelligence, but it's just another variable in the equation.

A carpeter would be equivalent to have a nice set of tools (this is the iq). BUT, if he does not put the effort, the risk to make mistakes, etc. he won't be a good carpeter. On the other hand, someone with a spoon and grit might end up creating awesome pieces of woodcraft. I think this analogy is pretty visual.

Same thing with effort, isolated is also not a good KPI. People that try to get perpetum mobile they spent a lot of effort and some of them where pretty smart. And it was a waste of time.

So effort and intelligence are necesary but not sufficient conditions to success. We need to focus on the objective achievements (what have you done that set you apart). That is, we don't remember Einstein because he was a smart guy, but because of his specific contributions to science and showing the tonge in funny pics.

When I do a tech interview to a candidate I put more weight on what verificable stuff you've done (conferences, github repos, bug bounty, certs, blog, etc.) over years of experience or standard education. I want a PoW that reflects passion and demonstrates that you can effectively solve problems.
